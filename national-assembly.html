National Assembly Information

About the National Assembly

The National Assembly is the supreme legislative body of the United Republic of Stars. Composed of 150 elected Representatives, the Assembly debates and passes all national legislation, approves the state budget, ratifies international treaties, and provides democratic oversight of the Executive branch.

Structure & Composition

The Assembly is divided into standing committees covering Finance, Defence, Justice, Health, Education, Infrastructure and Foreign Affairs. Each committee reviews legislation within its remit before it is put to a full Assembly vote. The Speaker presides over all plenary sessions and ensures parliamentary procedure.

Legislative Process

Bills may be introduced by the Government, by a committee, or by any Representative with the support of ten co-signatories. Once introduced, a bill undergoes three readings: introduction, committee review, and final vote. Passed bills are forwarded to the President for signature into law.

Sessions & Public Access

The Assembly meets in regular session from October to June. Extraordinary sessions may be called by the President or by petition of one third of Representatives. All plenary debates are publicly broadcast and transcripts are published in the Official Gazette within 48 hours.
